How Smallpox affected the Aztecs

Who brought Smallpox to the Aztecs? Smallpox was brought to the Aztecs by Spanish explorers. Smallpox alone killed about 5 to 8 million Aztecs after the battle of Tenochtitlan. In 1519 when Cortes came to the Aztecs, there were about 30 million Aztecs alive. Hundred years later after the Smallpox epidemics 1.5 to 3 million natives had survived this painful decade.
